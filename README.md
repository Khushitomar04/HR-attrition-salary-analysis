# HR-attrition-salary-analysis
Analysis of employee attrition and salary patterns using Python

## Overview
This project analyzes the IBM HR Analytics Employee Attrition dataset to understand 
the key factors driving employee attrition, with a focus on salary and overtime patterns.

## Dataset
- Source: Kaggle - IBM HR Analytics Employee Attrition Dataset
- 1,470 employee records, 35 features

## Tools Used
- Python (Pandas, Matplotlib)
- Google Colab

## Key Insights
- Employees in the Low salary band had a 29% attrition rate vs 9% for the Very High salary band
- Employees working overtime had a 31% attrition rate — 3x higher than employees who don't work overtime (10%)
- Employees who left had significantly lower average salary (~Rs 4,800) compared to those who stayed (~Rs 6,800)
- Sales department had the highest attrition rate compared to other departments

## Recommendations
- Design a targeted retention plan for low-salary band and Sales department employees
- Review overtime policy — consider workload balancing or additional compensation
- Conduct salary benchmarking for competitive pay structure
